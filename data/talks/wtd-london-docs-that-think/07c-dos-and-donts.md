# Do's and don'ts

Things we've learned the hard way.

---

# Do

- **Start with a skill, not a prompt** — A one-off prompt gets one-off quality. A skill gets consistency across every conversation.
- **Keep humans in the loop** — AI drafts, humans verify. Especially for anything customer-facing or technically precise.
- **Version-control your skills** — They're documentation about your documentation. Treat them like code.
- **Feed it your real content** — Upload style guides, glossaries, API specs. The more context, the better the output.
- **Start small** — One doc type. One workflow. Prove the value before scaling.

---

# Don't

- **Don't publish AI output without review** — It will hallucinate confidently. A wrong API example is worse than no example.
- **Don't use AI to replace understanding** — If you don't understand the product well enough to review the output, you're not ready.
- **Don't ignore tone** — Default AI writing sounds like AI writing. Your readers will notice.
- **Don't over-automate** — Not every doc task benefits from AI. Sometimes a human writing from scratch is faster and better.
- **Don't treat prompts as permanent** — Models change. Your product changes. Revisit and update your skills regularly.

---

# The golden rule

AI should make your good writers faster — not replace the need for good writers.

If the output needs less than 5 minutes of editing, it was worth it.
If it needs a full rewrite, your skill needs work — or the task wasn't right for AI.
